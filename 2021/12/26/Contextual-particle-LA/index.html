Contextual particle 'LA'

blog

Here are some examples of sentences using the “la“ particle.

Time:

  1. tenpo suno ni la, mi pilin lete.
    (I feel cold today.)
  2. tenpo open ni la tomo tawa suli li jo e jan mute.
    (This morning, the bus is full.)
  3. tenpo suno kama la, mi lukin e lipu ante.
    (tomorrow, I’ll read another book.)
  4. tenpo suno sike kama la, mi pali pi wawa mute.
    (Next year, I will work hard!)
  5. tenpo pini la, soweli mute li lon ma kasi suli.
    (There were many animals in the forest.)
  6. tenpo kama lili la, mi pona e tomo mi.
    (I’m going to fix my house soon.)
  7. tenpo ni la ona li kama ala kama?
    (Has it arrived yet?)

Adv. conjunction:

  1. sama la, mi pilin ike tawa ona.
    (I also feel sad for her.)
  2. nanpa tu la, o pana e kili ma lin ilo seli.
    (Secondly, put the potatoes on the stove.)
  3. kin la, tomo tawa sina li wawa.
    (Indeed, your car is powerful.)
  4. ken la, mi tu li tu.
    (We may break up).
  5. ante la, sina ken tawa lon tenpo suno kama.
    (Otherwise, you can leave tomorrow.)
  6. lon la, ni li toki ike taso.
    (I’m sure it’s just a misunderstanding.)

Refering (or emphasing) to things or persons:

  1. ni la, jan ala li wile kama.
    (Because of this, no one will want to come.)
  2. mi la, ni li ike mute.
    (From my perspective, this is unfair.)
  3. jan ni la, wile ike li lon tawa kasi nasa.
    (This person, he is addicted to tobacco)
  4. tan wile ona la, ona li kama sona e ijo mute.
    (Thanks to his motivation, he studied a lot of things.)
  5. moku la, jan Pusa li nanpa wan!
    (When it comes to gastronomy, jan Pusa is the best!)

Linking two propositions:

  1. sina pini la, o kama toki tawa mi.
    (When you’re done, come tell me about it.)
  2. ona li awen toki la, pilin mi li kama ike.
    (If he keeps talking, I’ll get mad.)
  3. mama tu sina li kama la, ni li pona tawa mi.
    (I’d be happy for your parents to come.)

Comparing things:

  1. kili la, pan li pona tawa sijelo.
    (Bread is better for your health than fruit.)
  2. pona tawa lukin la, len ni li nanpa wan.
    (This garment is prettier.)
  3. ilo oko tu la, nanpa wan li pona.
    (Of these two pairs of glasses, the first one is the better.)
